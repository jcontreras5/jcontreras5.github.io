Cycling

One of my hobbies and main forms of exercise and transportation is through cycling. I've always known how to ride a bicycle but I really got heavily reinvested into it in my early 20's. I purchased a fixed gear bicycle when I was about 21 to commute to my community college and part time job. I instantly fell in love with it as my primary method of transportation, weather permitting and all. I enjoy ebbing and flowing through traffic during rush hour stress free and exploring small branch off streets of the city. I tend to ride as long as it is not raining/ heavily, snow free, and the weather is above 30 degrees Fahrenheit.

I ride a fixed gear ,which is a single speed bicycle with very simple components, that makes repairs and diagnosing issues quick and easy. I also enjoy riding fixed due to the direct transfer of power to get the bike moving. The stronger you are the faster you will ride. There is no mechanical advantage like with riding a ten speed that has gears and shifters. I try to get at least one hundred miles in a week, when possible. The longest distance I've ridden is about 90 miles in one day from South Loop to Joliet.
